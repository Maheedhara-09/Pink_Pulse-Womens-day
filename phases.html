Phases of Menstrual Cycle

The menstrual cycle has four phases, and each phase affects the body differently. Hereâ€™s a simple way to understand them, along with diet and exercise tips for each phase.

1) Menstrual Phase (Period Days - 3 to 7 days)

What Happens? The body sheds the uterine lining, and bleeding occurs. Energy levels may be low.

  • Diet: Eat iron-rich foods like spinach, lentils, and dates to make up for blood loss. Drink warm soups and herbal teas to ease cramps.
  • Exercise: Do light activities like walking, stretching, or gentle yoga to help with cramps and bloating.

2) Follicular Phase (After Period - 7 to 10 days)

What Happens? The body starts preparing a new egg, and energy levels rise. Mood improves.

  • Diet: Eat protein-rich foods like eggs, fish, and nuts. Include leafy greens and berries for vitamins.
  • Exercise: Strength training, cardio workouts, and running are great since energy is high.

3) Ovulation Phase (Middle of the Cycle - 3 to 5 days)

What Happens? The body releases an egg, and energy is at its peak. You may feel your best.

  • Diet: Eat zinc-rich foods like pumpkin seeds and seafood. Stay hydrated and eat fresh fruits.
  • Exercise: High-intensity workouts, dancing, or sports are great as the body feels strong.

4) Luteal Phase (Before Period - 10 to 14 days)

What Happens? Hormones change, and PMS symptoms like mood swings or cravings may appear. Energy may drop.

  • Diet: Eat magnesium-rich foods like dark chocolate, bananas, and nuts to reduce bloating and mood swings. Reduce salty and sugary foods.
  • Exercise: Do yoga, pilates, or light workouts to manage stress and bloating. Avoid heavy workouts if feeling tired.
