What is Menopause?

Menopause is a natural phase in a womanâ€™s life when her periods stop permanently. It usually happens between the ages of 45 and 55. The body produces fewer reproductive hormones (like estrogen and progesterone), leading to changes like hot flashes, mood swings, and sleep problems.

Dealing with Menopause

Diet:

  • Eat calcium-rich foods (milk, yogurt, leafy greens) to keep bones strong.
  • Include protein (eggs, fish, beans) to support muscle health.
  • Drink plenty of water to prevent dryness and bloating.
  • Avoid caffeine, alcohol, and spicy foods if they trigger hot flashes.
  • Eat foods rich in healthy fats (nuts, seeds, and avocados) to balance hormones.

Exercise:

  • Walking or jogging to maintain heart health.
  • Yoga and meditation to manage stress and improve sleep.
  • Strength training to prevent bone loss.
  • Stretching to stay flexible and reduce joint pain.

Other Tips

  • Get enough sleep and follow a bedtime routine.
  • Wear light, breathable clothes to manage hot flashes.
  • Practice deep breathing or relaxation techniques to reduce stress.
  • Talk to a doctor if symptoms become too uncomfortable.
