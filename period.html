What is Menstruation?

Menstruation, also known as a period, is a normal and natural part of growing up for girls. It's something that happens inside their bodies as they become teenagers.

Why It Happens:

Each month, a girl's body prepares for a possible baby by making the lining of the uterus thick and cozy. If there's no baby, the body sheds this lining as blood.

How It Happens:

The blood leaves the body through the vagina. This process usually lasts 3 to 7 days and happens once a month.

Sign of Growing Up:

Having a period means a girl's body is healthy and growing. It's a normal part of becoming an adult.
